TAMIL NADU PAGRI

State of Origin:

Tamil Nadu

Introduction:

The traditional headgear worn by men donned during grand or auspicious ceremonies, it is compulsory to offer the dignitaries a traditional welcome in ceremonies by wearing a Pagri.

History:

The history of Pagri in Tamil Nadu dates back to the ancient times when it was worn as a symbol of pride and dignity by warriors and kings. Over time, it became a common accessory worn by men of all classes, and various styles and techniques were developed for tying the Pagri.

Design:

It is a long piece of cloth, usually made of cotton or silk, which is wrapped around the head in a specific style to form a turban.

Significance:

The Pagri holds great cultural and social significance in Indian society, including in Tamil Nadu.It indicates the symbol of pride
