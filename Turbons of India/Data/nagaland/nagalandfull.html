State of Origin:

Nagaland

Introduction:

The unique Naga headdress is synonymous with position, status and power one is wielding in the society The Nagas wear the Warriorâ€™s hat on the days of festivals along with lots of jewelry. Naga's hat, conical in form, has a fine patina and clear age.

History:

The Konyak warrior tribe is one of the many Naga tribes. But what sets them apart from the rest of the tribes of this north-eastern Indian state is their fierce headhunting history, which was part of their strong warrior tradition. they beheaded their enemies and brought back the severed heads as trophies in a specially designed basket that they carried to the battles. The heads were then proudly displayed on the walls and doorways of the warriors. This hat is wore by trible people while on war So, it named as warrior hat . it is a symbol of power and prestige. Decorated with red-colored human and goat hair, Ceremonial hats, embellished with animal horns and furs meant to evoke the power, courage, and beauty of the animals.

Design:

It is made from woven un-dyed cane base over which is fixed a mat of thin, red-dyed cane interspersed with the dried yellow cane which itself has an excellent, deep patina. A plume of red-dyed goat's hair is attached to a curved bamboo sliver that is covered in the dress of a Naga man. Size of hat: height: 34cm, length of base: 20cm.

Significance:

There are many Naga tribes living in NE India and their hat is suggestive of their place of residence. Hemi Naga people of Western Burma & North Eastern India follow similar tradition
