GUJARAT'S TURBAN

State of Origin:

GUJARAT

Introduction:

In Gujarat the turban is called Phento, which is basically a voluminous rolled Pagdi.The men in rural parts of Gujarat State of India usually wear the traditional thickly folded turban on their head .Bright Colors are generally chosen for the Phento which looks very attractive

History:

In earlier times people didn't have to ask where you came from. Because the turban worn on the man's head told everything. There were more than 20 different types of turbans including Kathiawadi Halai, Kutchi, Laria of seven different colors, Rajasthani, Jalawadi, Gohilwad, Sorathi. In which red turban was worn on good occasions and white turban on mourning occasions. people wear it in famous Garba dance of Navratri.

Design:

Pento is a layered cloth wrapped around the head. It has very vibrant and attractive colour , Yellow , red, orange, green, blue are most commonly used colour . People in kutch wear it under long sleeved jackets beneath the coat. The men usually wear white coloured clothes with red or white coloured phento or turban on their heads.

Significance:

The state of Gujaratis also known as the ‘Jewel of Western India’ and the traditional dress of the state reflects its rich culture and heritage. The traditional dress of Gujarat is different for both men and women and there is a certain kind of diversity in the costumes. This is because there are various ethnic groups constituting Gujarat’s population.”
