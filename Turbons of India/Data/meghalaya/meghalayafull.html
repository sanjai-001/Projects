MEGHALAYA'S BEHDEINKHLAM

State of Origin:

Meghalaya

Introduction:

One of Meghalaya's most colourful religious festivals, Behdeinkhlam, is celebrated for three days during July at Jowai. The male wear a beautiful red turban of special mulberry or Eri silk shawls of Meghalaya .

History:

Behdeinkhlam , This is the most important festival of the Jaintias and is celebrated after the sowing is done. It is a popular and colourful festival .It is a tradition for Khasi men to wear a turban on special occasions. All of them are adept at wrapping this on quickly and nicely. They wear red turbans . These are made of mulberry silk or sometimes it is made up of Eri Silk Shawls . During the dancing rituals of Ka Pom-Blang Nongkrem festivities, the male dancer of the tribe wear a beautiful turban made of silk alongside a gold or silver semi-circular plate around the neck. The tribe carry a sword in one hand.

Design:

There are two types of turbans worn on different occasions. Turbans using mulberry silk shawls: These are of mixed colors, like yellow, red, and maroon. These are worn during dance festivals, wedding ceremonies and on other festive occasions. Turbans using Eri silk shawls: These are made using hand woven silk shawls made of Eri silk and worn while performing rituals, like in the Behdeinkhlam festival. The same material is draped around the neck as a shawl during funerals. These are usually white in colour.

Significance:

The word Behdeinkhlam literally means 'driving away of evil (plague) by wooden sticks . The people of Meghalaya are incredibly proud of their heritage and their rich variety of clothing is a beautiful example of this heritage.Eri silk shawl is worn by Khasi men during weddings and funeral ceremonies as a mark of respect and as a symbol of solemnity.
